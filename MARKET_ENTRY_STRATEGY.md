## Executive Summary

This document maps EvoMind's **Decision Support Pack (DSP-01)** capability to specific entry points in government and enterprise markets. The strategy prioritizes **trust-building through constraint** rather than capability expansion.

### Strategic Principle

> "You do not sell AGI. You sell a constrained capability that reduces cost and risk."

### 1.3 Government Sales Approach

#### Step 1: Identify Champion (Weeks 1-4)

```
Target Persona: Mid-level Program Manager or Technical Lead
├── Pain: Drowning in analysis, slow decisions
├── Authority: Can request pilots
├── Budget: Has discretionary funds (<$100K)
└── Timeline: Needs results in months, not years
```

#### Step 2: Demonstrate Value (Weeks 4-8)

**Pilot Structure:**

| Phase | Duration | Deliverable |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Setup** | 1 week | Environment configured |
| **Pilot Run** | 2-4 weeks | 3-5 decision memos |
| **Assessment** | 1 week | Value report |

**Success Metrics:**

- Time to decision reduced by X%
- Documentation quality improved
- Evidence traceability established

#### Step 3: Expand (Months 3-12)

```
Expansion Path
├── Additional use cases within office
├── Adjacent offices in same organization  
├── Cross-agency referrals
└── Contract vehicle establishment
```

## Part 3: Competitive Positioning

### 3.1 Differentiation Matrix

| Dimension | LLM Wrappers | Consulting | DSP-01 |
|-----------|--------------|------------|--------|
| **Speed** | Fast | Slow | Fast |
| **Consistency** | Variable | Variable | High |
| **Evidence Mapping** | No | Manual | Automatic |
| **Audit Trail** | No | Partial | Complete |
| **Governance** | No | Process-based | Architectural |
| **On-Prem** | Rarely | N/A | Yes |
| **Cost** | Low | High | Medium |

### 3.2 Competitive Responses

**vs. "We'll just use ChatGPT"**
> "ChatGPT doesn't provide evidence mapping, can't deploy on-prem, and doesn't maintain governance boundaries. DSP-01 is architecturally governed—constraints are enforced, not suggested."

**vs. "We'll hire consultants"**
> "Consultants provide expertise but not repeatability. DSP-01 provides consistent, auditable outputs at lower cost and higher velocity. Use consultants for novel strategy; use DSP-01 for structured decisions."

**vs. "We'll build our own"**
> "Building governed cognition takes years. Our verification tests prove architectural properties that are extremely difficult to achieve. DSP-01 is ready now."

## Part 4: Pricing Strategy

### 4.1 Government Pricing

| Model | Description | Typical Range |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Pilot** | Fixed scope, fixed price | $25K-$100K |
| **Subscription** | Per-seat annual | $2K-$5K/seat/year |
| **Per-Deliverable** | Per decision memo | $500-$2K/memo |
| **Enterprise** | Unlimited use, fixed term | $100K-$500K/year |

### 4.2 Enterprise Pricing

| Model | Description | Typical Range |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Pilot** | Proof of value | $25K-$75K |
| **Department** | Single team | $50K-$150K/year |
| **Division** | Multiple teams | $150K-$500K/year |
| **Enterprise** | Organization-wide | $500K-$2M/year |

### 4.3 Pricing Principles

1. **Value-based, not cost-based** — Price on outcome, not compute
2. **Pilot-to-production path** — Easy entry, clear expansion
3. **No nickel-and-diming** — Predictable costs
4. **Premium for constraints** — Governance is a feature, not a limitation

## Part 6: Risk Mitigation

### 6.1 Market Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| "AI hype backlash" | Position on verification, not hype |
| "Competitor commoditization" | Differentiate on governance |
| "Regulatory uncertainty" | Design for constraint, not avoidance |

### 6.2 Delivery Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| "Pilot failure" | Conservative scope, clear success metrics |
| "Capability creep" | Strict envelope enforcement |
| "Data incident" | On-prem default, session-scoped |

### 6.3 Organizational Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| "Loss of core control" | Two-entity structure |
| "Over-expansion" | Capacity limits in contracts |
| "Mission drift" | Governance at Entity A level |
